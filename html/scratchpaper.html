About Rugby

Rugby is a fast-paced and thrilling sport that combines skill, strategy, and physical endurance. Originating in England in the early 19th century, rugby has grown into a global phenomenon with millions of players and fans worldwide.

Whether you're watching a match of Rugby Union or Rugby League, the energy on the field is unmatched. Each version of the game has its unique rules and fan base, but both share the essence of teamwork and determination.

  1. Key Features of Rugby
    • Two teams compete to score tries and goals.
    • A rugby ball is oval-shaped, unlike a traditional round ball.
    • Players can pass the ball backward or kick it forward.
  2. Major Rugby Tournaments
    • Rugby World Cup
    • Six Nations Championship
    • Super Rugby

Rules of the Game

Rugby is played with two teams of 15 players in Rugby Union and 13 players in Rugby League. The objective is to score more points than the opposing team by grounding the ball in the in-goal area (a try) or kicking it through the goalposts.

  • Players can run with the ball, but must pass it backward.
  • A match consists of two halves, each lasting 40 minutes.
  • Physical tackles are allowed, but high tackles and dangerous play are penalized.
